Principal Investigators

Dr Florian G. Kern

Project Lead (PI)

Reader, Department of Government
University of Essex

Florian is a Reader in the Department of Government at the University of Essex, where he also serves as Director of Impact. He is an internationally recognised expert in applied qualitative methods, secondary qualitative data analysis, and research transparency, with extensive fieldwork experience across Africa and a publication record spanning qualitative methods, African politics, and research ethics. The University of Essex is home to the UK Data Archive, making it a particularly fitting base for this project.

fkern@essex.ac.uk

Dr Christian Arnold

Project Co-Lead (Co-PI)

Associate Professor in Government and Artificial Intelligence
University of Birmingham

Christian is an Associate Professor in the School of Government at the University of Birmingham and a member of the University's Institute for Data and AI and its Centre for AI in Government. His work bridges machine learning and qualitative inquiry, with recent projects applying large language models and multimodal analysis to parliamentary debate, political negotiations, and electoral data. He brings both technical depth in computational methods and practical experience in qualitative research design.

c.arnold@bham.ac.uk

Research Software Engineers

The PIs are supported by research software engineers at the University of Birmingham with experience in large-scale, data-intensive computational social science projects.
